  • Words of Advice

    I think the best thing about being in Formula Student Racing is how it prepares you for real life experiences. I know how lame it sounds and you hear stuff like that from alot of people, but it's true. It teaches you how extensive the design process actually is, not to mention what it takes to fabricate one of those things! It teaches you things that you don't learn in classes - how to network and raise funds, how to cost out a prototype, how to machine or layup composite parts, and the list goes on... And, it's the only real way to get a job in the automotive or racing industry because being in this program literally puts you miles ahead of the competition. We are Nequit family and we are with you to help in technology transformation, planning and management guidance and whatever team needs. One thing I forgot to mention. Even if you pass out, if you are passionate enough you won't be able to leave Race Car. I was called to be the Judge at Formula Bharat and SUPRA. Yeah tha's how the legacy continues. All the best for success... We are not designed to come second or third, we are designed to win....Never Quit.....BecauseRacCar...!

Abhishek More

Captain - Nequit 4.0 for Formula Bharat
Lead - Engine and Powertrain.
  • Words of Advice

    I joined the team in my second year and was a member until I passed out. After joining Team Nequit I started working in Engine department. I used to read different design reports in order to understand various design aspects. I was very curious to understand different systems and there design procedures. Our Captain Pranav used to send me to different places to meet business people and convince them to give sponsorship. I used to accompany Ravi in this adventure. At that time I was very shy person and did not know how to communicate, on the other hand Ravi was excellent in convincing people. He used to do all the talking and I used to just observe him. Sometimes we returned empty handed and sometimes we were successful. Slowly I started coming out of my comfort zone and started supporting Ravi. These were my first lessons on effective communication which I learned from my teammate. Being part of the team gives you this opportunity to learn from observing your teammates. Team forces you to collaborate with others and observe their best qualities. I was very lucky to be surrounded by excellent teammates who taught me many skills which are part of my life. When I started working as Engine and Powertrain head we used to face many problems in our system. To get to know these problems and to resolve them was very challenging job. It helped me to develop very important problem solving skills. We learned how to look at a problem from all the perspective in order to reach to the root of that problem. Till date I use this technique to resolve the issues in front of me. I got an opportunity to improve my leadership skills when I became the Captain of team. We had a challenge to participate and perform for the first time at Formula Bharat. Because of the hard work from team members, we performed excellent in that event. I was fortunate to be the driver of the team also; along with my co-driver Onkar we completed the endurance race of 21 kms. We also faced many tense situations during the event from which I learned how to remain calm in tensed conditions. It was a memorable event. Being part of the team is a big learning opportunity. You get to learn many skills which otherwise you will never learn just being a student. From each team member you can learn something; from each situation you can learn something. In your life you will face many difficult challenges, performing well in team will give you the confidence to take on those challenges. Do your best and Never Quit.

Jaykumar Bankar

Captain - Formula Bharat 2019.

Catia QA Associate Engineer.
Dassault Systems Solutions Lab, Pune.
  • Words of Advice

    I joined the team in my third year of engineering, but desire to join the team was from second year as I saw captain Pranav driving gravity 2.0 in college campus. That moment I decided to join the team. I started my FS journey with “Team Nequit” for new season of Formula Bharat in suspension department with enormous amount of passion. It was challenging task for suspension team to build a system with “zero compliance”. Here I understood the hard work required for a single task to be done, which in turns adds to team’s goals. I still remember those sleepless nights of diwali vacation, where we were working to get things done for photographic submission. At the FB2018 event (my first event), I felt amazing as well as worrying at the same time by looking at other teams and cars. The environment of event was very enthusiastic and everyone out there was full of passion towards formula student. We cleared all necessary tests and completed endurance and won Rookie team of the year award. Every member felt that energy of cheering “Nequit-Nequit” while receiving the award. In SUPRA’18 season, we performed very well in all aspects. That was because of captain Shubham Yadav. He planned and executed things very well. He taught me discipline, team management skills, decision making, task and time managing skills and many more. He brought confidence in me. For the next Formula Bharat season, I was assigned the role of Captain by Shubham Yadav. I was surprised at first moment but took this as great opportunity and responsibility. This was the turning point of my engineering and professional career. It was very challenging for me and team as well to design, build and test the vehicle in six months. The team was structured well and highly motivated. The journey started well and some tasks completed as planned, some with tolerance in deadline. We met all document submission deadlines and in only 5 months the car was on four wheels!!! Amazing! I think no one form that team will forget the period of 5 months. But unfortunately, we ran into big engine problem and that costs us dynamic events at the competition. I thank all the members of that team who supported and backed up team in those situations. After this failure, it was difficult to maintain team’s bonding. Captain Rupesh managed well and team was up for the next event SUPRA’19. This time team was very young and less experienced. So, I decided to be with team in this time. This was my last event and also last chance to fulfill the dream of completing all dynamic events and endurance at BIC, Delhi. The event dates were extended and they overlapped with joining dates of company in which I was placed. I remember those inspirational words form Shubham Yadav - “Go for the event, don’t think about job, you will get better in coming time”. He was right and I went. We cleared TI, tilt, noise in first day of the event and brakes test in following day. I was the driver for Skidpad and Auto-X. I felt that pressure as every team’s driver feels. The skidpad went very well but Auto-x was not upto the expectations. The next day was endurance day. With excitement and fear in mind, I and Co-driver Sourabh Pawar went on track. There was only one thing in mind, ‘At any cost, I have to complete the race’. And we did it. Team Nequit completed endurance at BIC for the very first time with 6th rank in race. The unforgettable day in my life. I heard many times, ‘never give up, never quit’. But, in real life I went through it. There are many things I learned, core technical skills and soft skills in FS journey with Team Nequit. But, this is something different, I learned and experienced that - as the name suggests, “Never ever quit!!! NEQUIT”. I continued my FS passion and won’t be able to leave race car thing. I am Junior Tech Inspector at Formula Bharat and now also as Document reviewer. At the end, All the best for the current team members, believe in yourself and team. Always keep in mind, “If you can’t stop thinking about it, don’t stop working for it”
